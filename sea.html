🌊 Bioaccumulation in the Sea 🐟

What is Bioaccumulation?

When harmful chemicals enter the ocean, small fish absorb them. Then bigger fish eat the small ones, collecting more toxins. Finally, top predators like tuna or sharks carry the highest levels. And when humans eat seafood, those same toxins enter our bodies.

This process is called bioaccumulation. It shows how pollution doesn’t just stay in the water — it climbs the food chain and affects all of us.

The Beauty of the Sea 🌊✨

The sea is more than just water. It is a world of life — colorful fish, playful dolphins, giant whales, and coral reefs that shine like underwater gardens.

It gives us food, fresh air, and peace. Standing by the shore, listening to the waves, feels like the ocean is speaking to us, reminding us how big and beautiful nature really is.

The sea connects countries, cultures, and people. It is a gift we should always admire and protect.

Protecting the sea means protecting ourselves 💙
